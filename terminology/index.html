Terminology: Those who promote pseudoscience, such as diet programs or other products, often use terminology that is vague, misleading, or scientifically unfounded. Here are some examples:

"Detox"

This term is often used to market diets, supplements, or other products that claim to rid the body of toxins. However, there is little scientific evidence to support the idea that these products can actually detoxify the body, and the term itself is vague and unscientific.

"Proven"

This term is often used to market products or diets that claim to have been scientifically proven to work. However, the term is often used in a way that is misleading, and may not accurately represent the quality or quantity of evidence supporting the product or diet in question.

"Revolutionary"

This has become the most popular of the buzzwords to describe some product that is “pioneering” or “leading the way”. High-tech products with little testing that may be early in development use these phrases to get around the fact that their product is not completely developed or is one of few in its field of development.

"Natural/ Healing powers"

"Experience the healing power of plasma energy with your Plasma Health Pen. Our highly sought-after Plasma Health Pen is a game-changer in the world of natural healing."
